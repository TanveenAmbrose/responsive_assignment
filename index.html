Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

sun damage and fading could indicate that you've had your helmet for a long time & therefore is time to switch it up. Things like scuffs and cracks are also a fair indication of extensive wear & damage, meaning you will need to replace your helmet.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If the bike is well-maintained and its components are in good working order, it can be just as safe as a new bike. However, if the bike is in poor condition or has worn-out components, it may not be safe to ride.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a helmet on every ride. Wear bright, highly visible clothing, preferably with reflective tape or patches. Obey the rules of the road. ... Ride with the flow of traffic, not against it. Ride in control at all times. ... Yield to pedestrians and other vehicles.
